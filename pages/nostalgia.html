Content Index

  • Windows 93 (Interactive OS Simulator) - A fully functional, trippy recreation of classic Windows in your browser. Complete with games, apps, and Easter eggs.
  • Space Jam (1996) Official Website (Preserved Website) - The original Space Jam movie website, unchanged since 1996. Peak 90s web design with frames, GIFs, and Comic Sans.
  • Hampster Dance (Meme Site) - One of the first viral internet phenomena. Dancing hamsters with looping music. Pure 90s chaos.
  • Windows 95 in Browser (OS Emulator) - Run the actual Windows 95 operating system in your browser. Includes Minesweeper, Paint, Solitaire.
  • Old Reddit (Classic UI) - The original Reddit interface before the redesign. Minimal, fast, information-dense.
  • Geocities Archive (Website Archive) - Browse archived Geocities websites. Peak personal web design from the early internet era.
  • The Eternal Maze Screensaver (Screensaver Recreation) - The iconic 3D maze screensaver from Windows 95. Procedurally generated endless hallways.
  • Poolside FM (Retro Music Radio) - Vaporwave-inspired internet radio with retro Mac aesthetic. Summer vibes and nostalgic music.
  • Webamp - Winamp in Browser (Music Player) - Faithful recreation of Winamp 2.9 in the browser. It really whips the llama's ass.
  • Internet Archive - MS-DOS Games (Game Archive) - Thousands of DOS games playable directly in your browser. Oregon Trail, Prince of Persia, Doom.
  • Zombo.com (Experimental Site) - Welcome to Zombocom. This is Zombocom. Welcome. The only limit is yourself. An internet mystery.
  • The Useless Web (Random Site Generator) - Takes you to random useless but entertaining websites. Captures the playful spirit of early internet.
  • Windows XP Simulator (OS Simulator) - Experience Windows XP again. Includes the iconic startup sound, Bliss wallpaper, and classic UI.
  • Y2K Aesthetic Institute (Cultural Archive) - Digital culture archive exploring Y2K era design, technology, and aesthetics. Glossy, futuristic, optimistic.
  • One Terabyte of Kilobyte Age (Geocities Archive Project) - Tumblr showcasing screenshots from old Geocities pages. A museum of amateur web design.
  • AOL Instant Messenger Revival (Chat Client) - Fan-made revival of AIM. Relive the door slam sound and away messages.
  • The Million Dollar Homepage (Historical Website) - A student sold 1 million pixels for $1 each. Peak mid-2000s internet entrepreneurship.
  • Flash Game Archive - Flashpoint (Game Preservation) - Preserving 100,000+ Flash games and animations. Download to play classics from Newgrounds, Miniclip.
  • Neopets Classic (Virtual Pet Game) - The original virtual pet site, still running. Feed your Neopet and play Flash-free games.
  • FFFFOUND! Archive (Image Bookmarking) - Iconic invite-only image bookmarking site. Frozen in time but archives still viewable.
  • Club Penguin Rewritten (Game Revival) - Fan-made recreation of Club Penguin after Disney shut it down. Waddle on!
  • Wayback Machine (Web Archive) - Browse 866 billion archived web pages. See any website as it looked in the past.
  • ASCII Art Archive (Text Art Collection) - Massive collection of ASCII art from BBS days and early internet. Copy-paste nostalgia.
  • Old Games Download (Game Archive) - Free downloads of classic PC games. DOS, Windows 95/98 era games.
  • Textfiles.com (Text Archive) - Archive of BBS textfiles. Hacker manifestos, phreaking guides, ANSI art. Raw internet history.
  • Neocities (Modern Geocities) - Free web hosting inspired by Geocities. Modern take on personal homepages and creative freedom.
